PubSearch Data and Dataflow

Publications

TAIR’s PubSearch installation presently contains 15,486 research articles, book chapters, and reviews (7,782 full-text), and 1,204 abstracts from conference proceedings. Most article information is obtained from (PubMed, monthly download) and the National Agronomics Library (NAL, quarterly download) that compiles both Agricola and BIOSIS. The criteria for retrieving articles is the occurrence of the word 'Arabidopsis’ in the title, abstract or keywords; this query can easily be adapted for use with any other organism or purpose of a database. The conference abstracts are usually obtained from the conference organizers directly. Where possible, full text articles are downloaded in portable document format (PDF) or scanned using optical character recognition software Omnipage 11 and converted to PDF. About 20% of the articles in PDF were converted into plain text using the pdftotext utility available on Unix platforms. These plain text representations of articles are used in the full text search.

Loci and genes

The main source of gene information was obtained from the TAIR database Updates come mainly from Genbank, TIGR and the user community. New genes are also identified by PubSearch by scanning the literature using regular expressions specific for gene symbols. Presently, there are 31,218 loci and 37,240 genes in the PubSearch database.

Keywords

Keywords comprise approximately 14,238 Gene Ontology terms, and 400 terms from the TAIR ontologies (developmental stages and anatomy of Arabidopsis The GO terms are being updated in a local installation of the GO database on a daily basis from the Gene Ontology CVS repository. The PubSearch term table is then updated from the local GO database.
